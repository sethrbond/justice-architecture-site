Criminal Justice
Administrative Backlogs Are Keeping People Out of the Workforce

Millions of Americans carry criminal records legally eligible for expungement but remain on file due to paper-based processes — blocking employment, housing, and civic participation.

Immigration & Due Process
Frontline Organizations Are Overwhelmed and Under-Resourced

Accelerating enforcement actions, growing deportation dockets, and overwhelmed legal clinics have created a crisis in due process. The organizations defending asylum seekers and families face unprecedented pressure.

The Problem
Why This Work Matters Now

Two systems are failing at once — criminal justice administrative backlogs and immigration due process. The legal infrastructure designed to protect people's rights is collapsing under its own weight.

Organizational Structure

Two Entities. One Mission.

A 501(c)(3) innovation laboratory paired with a 501(c)(4) advocacy engine, governed by strict legal and financial separation.

501(c)(3) — Tax-Deductible
The Justice Architecture Fund
Innovation & Stabilization

Deploys capacity-building grants through the Discovery Protocol. Conducts 90-day operational audits to map systemic bottlenecks. Will eventually fund open-source legal infrastructure.

Active. Deploying Discovery Protocol grants to vetted frontline organizations.
501(c)(4) — Social Welfare
Justice Architecture Action
Policy & Advocacy

Provides legislative and political support to implement systemic reforms — lobbying for administrative modernization and translating technical pilots into law.

Paused Q1/Q2 2026. All resources prioritized toward the 501(c)(3) pilot. Advocacy activates once the model is proven.

The Justice Architecture Fund is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization registered in Washington State. Contributions are tax-deductible to the extent allowed by law.

Justice Architecture Action is a 501(c)(4) social welfare organization. Contributions are not tax-deductible as charitable donations.

Both entities operate under a formal Resource Sharing Agreement and maintain separate governance, bank accounts, and financial records.
