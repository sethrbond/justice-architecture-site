The Dual-Entity Model

Built for complementary power.

Most nonprofits are limited to either charitable work or political advocacy. We do both — through two legally separate entities that share resources under strict IRS-compliant terms.

501(c)(3) — Tax-Deductible
The Justice Architecture Fund
Innovation & Stabilization

The operational engine. Deploys capacity-building grants to frontline organizations through the Discovery Protocol. Conducts 90-day operational audits to map systemic bottlenecks. Will eventually fund open-source legal infrastructure tools informed by field data. All contributions are tax-deductible.

Status: Active. Deploying Discovery Protocol grants to vetted frontline organizations. Seed capital funded. Infrastructure buildout underway.
501(c)(4) — Social Welfare
Justice Architecture Action
Policy & Advocacy

The political arm. Provides legislative and political support to implement systemic reforms — lobbying for administrative modernization, supporting reform candidates, and translating technical pilots from the Fund into law and policy. Contributions are not tax-deductible.

Status: Paused Q1/Q2 2026. By unanimous Board resolution, all resources are prioritized toward the 501(c)(3) pilot model. The c4 reactivates once the Discovery Protocol is validated.

Legal Architecture

The firewall that makes it work.

The two entities operate under a formal Resource Sharing Agreement (RSA) — a legal document that governs exactly how resources, staff time, and office infrastructure are shared between the 501(c)(3) and 501(c)(4).

The Fund (c3) serves as the "Provider" entity and is reimbursed at fair market value for any resources shared with Action (c4). This prevents prohibited "private inurement" — ensuring that tax-deductible charitable contributions never subsidize political advocacy work.

What's Separated

  • Separate bank accounts (dual Chase Business Checking)
  • Separate governance (independent Board oversight)
  • Separate financial records (independent QuickBooks instances)
  • Separate program operations (no comingling of grant capital)

What's Shared (Under RSA Terms)

  • Office space and digital infrastructure (reimbursed at FMV)
  • Staff time where legally permissible (tracked and allocated)
  • Strategic intelligence (bottleneck data informs both charitable programs and policy advocacy)
Governance

Active oversight. Not passive compliance.

Our Board of Directors consists of independent voting members who actively participate in operational planning, capital deployment decisions, and strategic direction. This is not a passive advisory board — members review every grant through the formal Vetting Matrix and vote on all capital deployments.

Board Responsibilities

  • Review and vote on all Discovery Protocol grant deployments via the three-tier Vetting Matrix
  • Analyze Monthly Executive Flash Reports covering pipeline movement, capital burn rates, and technology intelligence
  • Ensure the dual-entity firewall remains intact through quarterly Resource Sharing Agreement reviews
  • Execute the Conflict of Interest Policy annually
  • Vote on annual operating budgets for both entities

Key Board Resolutions (Q1 2026)

  • Ratified organization-to-organization grant model exclusively — no direct-to-individual grants
  • Delayed technology development until Discovery Protocol validates bottleneck data
  • Paused 501(c)(4) operations for Q1/Q2 2026 to concentrate resources on the pilot
  • Confirmed seed capital balance for pilot deployment
  • Directed development of the Master Dossier and Operational Roadmap
