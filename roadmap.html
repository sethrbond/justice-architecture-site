Where we've been. Where we're going.

Our operational roadmap is phased deliberately. We don't build tools before understanding the problem. We don't scale advocacy before proving the model.

Operational Timeline

Phased by design. Not by hesitation.

Justice Architecture follows a deliberate, evidence-driven roadmap. Each phase must be validated before the next one activates. This isn't caution — it's engineering discipline applied to social change.

Dec 2025
Entity Formation & Legal Architecture

Incorporated both entities in Washington State. Filed IRS Form 1023 (c3) and Form 8976 (c4). Adopted bylaws, Conflict of Interest Policy, and Resource Sharing Agreement. Established dual Chase Business Checking accounts. Set up Google Workspace, domain, and digital infrastructure.

Jan – Feb 2026
Infrastructure Buildout & Governance

Developed the Master Dossier and Operational Roadmap. Built the "Moral Mathematics" database — Partner Pipeline, Capital Ledger, Bottleneck Repository. Created the three-tier Partner Vetting Matrix and Discovery Protocol interview methodology. Held Q1 Joint Board Meeting — ratified org-to-org model, paused c4, confirmed seed capital for pilot deployment. Onboarded Board members with operational mandates.

Q1 – Q2 2026
Discovery Protocol — Pilot Deployment

Executing the first Discovery Protocol grant cycles. Deploying capacity-building grants to vetted frontline organizations in priority states. Running the 45/75/90-day M.E.L. monitoring cycle. Mapping operational bottlenecks through the Micro-Report and System Mapping sessions. Populating the Bottleneck Repository with validated field intelligence.

H2 2026
Scale & Validate

Expand Discovery Protocol deployments across all eight priority states based on pilot data. Assess c4 reactivation based on legislative landscape and operational bandwidth. Begin synthesis of Bottleneck Repository data to identify the highest-impact intervention points for technology development. Pursue additional fundraising to expand grant capacity.

2027+
Technology Development & Systemic Scale

Fund boutique engineering teams to build open-source software tools that solve the exact bottlenecks documented through Discovery Protocol cycles. Deploy tools to partner organizations first, then release as open-source infrastructure for the broader legal ecosystem. Reactivate the c4 advocacy arm to translate technical pilots into legislative and policy reform.

Operating Principles

How we decide when to move.

Evidence Before Scale

We don't expand to new states, new grant sizes, or new program areas until the existing data validates the approach. The Discovery Protocol is designed to generate that evidence — which is why we deploy grants before building tools, and why we listen before we build.

Frontal Honesty Over Polished Narratives

If the Discovery Protocol reveals that our assumptions about bottlenecks were wrong, we pivot. If a partner organization tells us the tools we're planning to build wouldn't actually help, we redesign. The intelligence we collect is only valuable if we're willing to act on it — even when it contradicts our original thesis.

Discipline Over Speed

The deliberate pacing of our roadmap is not risk aversion — it's engineering discipline. Building the wrong tool is worse than building no tool at all. Deploying capital to the wrong organization is worse than holding reserves. Every phase must earn its way to the next.
