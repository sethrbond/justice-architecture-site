For Funders
Fund the Frontline

Contributions to The Justice Architecture Fund are tax-deductible under Section 501(c)(3) and directly fund capacity-building grants to frontline legal and immigration organizations. Every dollar deployed through the Discovery Protocol simultaneously stabilizes operations and generates the system intelligence that will inform our long-term tools.

For Organizations
Partner With Us

If you're a 501(c)(3) organization providing legal defense, asylum representation, or immigration services and you're facing operational bottlenecks — we want to hear from you. Our Discovery Protocol doesn't start with an application. It starts with a conversation.

What We Look For
Active 501(c)(3) status. Frontline legal focus. Geographic presence in our eight priority states. Willingness to collaborate on system mapping.

There is no formal application. Reach out and we'll schedule an informal Discovery Protocol interview.

Transparency

What your dollar actually does.

We operate on a trust-based model — which means we don't impose restrictive overhead ratios on our grantees and we won't impose vanity metrics on our donors. Here's what we will tell you:

Where Capital Goes

Grant capital goes directly to vetted 501(c)(3) partner organizations. Operational overhead for Justice Architecture itself is funded separately through seed contributions from our founding board.

How We Track It

Every grant is tracked through our Capital Ledger — documenting which organization received funds, what operational gap was addressed, and the measurable outcomes from the 90-day cycle. We don't publish this data publicly (to protect our grantees' operational privacy), but it's available to donors on request.

How Decisions Are Made

Every grant deployment is voted on by our Board of Directors after the organization passes our three-tier vetting process and clears the Tri-Gate accountability framework. No single individual controls capital deployment.

"This isn't charity. It's infrastructure investment — in the systems that protect people's rights."

The Justice Architecture Fund is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization registered in Washington State. Contributions are tax-deductible to the extent allowed by law.

Both entities operate under a formal Resource Sharing Agreement and maintain separate governance, bank accounts, and financial records.
