The Discovery Protocol: Listen before you build.

We deploy trust-based grants that serve a dual purpose: immediately stabilize frontline operations, and earn the trust required to map the exact systemic bottlenecks our future tools will solve.

Philosophy

Why we don't just write checks.

Most philanthropic organizations either write checks with no follow-up or impose rigid reporting requirements that consume the very resources they're trying to provide. We do neither.

Our approach is rooted in Trust-Based Philanthropy. We do not evaluate organizations based on restrictive metrics like "overhead ratios," which penalize grassroots entities for paying staff living wages. Instead, we deploy flexible capital and earn the right to learn from our grantees — not the other way around.

Every dollar we deploy is also an investment in intelligence. The operational friction we document through our Discovery Protocol directly informs the open-source tools we will eventually build. No other funder in this space is running this dual-purpose model.

The 90-Day Grant Cycle

Three touchpoints. Zero burden.

Each grant follows a structured but lightweight 90-day monitoring, evaluation, and learning (M.E.L.) cycle. The grantee sets the pace — we adapt to their schedule, not the other way around.

Day 1 – 45
Deploy & Stabilize

Flexible capital hits the ground — covering intake staff, filing fees, interpreter costs, or whatever operational gap the agency identifies as most urgent. No prescriptive restrictions on use. At Day 45, we send a brief informal email to confirm funds arrived and identify any immediate roadblocks. No formal report required.

Pulse Check → Informal Email
Day 45 – 75
Map & Validate

With trust established, we deploy a four-question Micro-Report survey: How were funds allocated? What operational improvements resulted? What systemic bottleneck remains? If you could redesign one workflow, what would it be? No lengthy reports. No overhead burden.

Micro-Report → 4-Question Survey
Day 75 – 90
Discover & Document

A collaborative 30–45 minute working session over Zoom. We validate the Micro-Report data, then ask the grantee to walk us through their actual intake workflow — screen sharing, spreadsheets, whatever they're comfortable showing. We look for manual data entry, broken integrations, and redundant paperwork.

System Mapping → 30-45 Min Working Session
What We Learn

Every grant generates system intelligence.

After each 90-day cycle, we integrate the data into three internal systems that collectively form our "Moral Mathematics" database:

Intelligence Layer
Bottleneck Repository

A tagged, searchable catalog of every operational friction point discovered — intake delays, software limitations, language translation gaps, lawyer assignment bottlenecks. Each entry is tagged by type and assessed for whether custom software could solve it.

Financial Layer
Capital Ledger

Tracks every dollar deployed — which organization, what operational gap it addressed, and the measurable outcome. Grant cycles are marked "Active" or "Closed" with full audit trail.

Relationship Layer
Partner Pipeline

Maps every potential and active grantee through the vetting process — from initial contact through Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3 evaluation.

Partner Selection

Three tiers. No shortcuts.

Every potential grantee is evaluated through a sequential three-tier system before funds are deployed. This protects our fiduciary duty while honoring the trust-based model.

Pass / Fail
Tier 1: Fiduciary Firewall

Strict legal threshold. Verifies active 501(c)(3) status, clean financial history, no pending legal action, and confirmed geographic presence in our target regions. Failure is immediate disqualification.

Scored 1–3
Tier 2: Strategic Alignment

Evaluates frontline legal focus (crimmigration, deportation defense, asylum), geographic priority within our eight core states, and target demographic alignment with vulnerable immigrant populations.

Scored 1–3
Tier 3: Discovery Potential

Assesses bottleneck clarity (can they articulate their operational friction?), technology receptivity (will they collaborate on system mapping?), and innovation receptivity (are they open to new tools?).

Scoring Threshold
Organizations must score 15/18 or higher across Tiers 2 and 3 to qualify for standard grants. Pilot grants — the first deployments — require 17-18/18 to ensure the highest-quality initial data.

"We're not auditing expenses. We're mapping the plumbing — so we can eventually fix it for everyone."
